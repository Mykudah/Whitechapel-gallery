Image 1: Sculpture and performance
Oil Hiranya Garbha

Title: Hiranya Garbha
Year made: 2012
Dimensions: 70 cm
Material: Sculpture with resin and paint, accompanied by a one-time performance
This sculpture was exhibited in Covent Garden for 40 days in 2012 and subsequently auctioned for charity. Before the auction, a live performance opposite St. Paul’s Churchyard, featured the egg being painted in front of an audience.
The performance symbolised birth. Part of a collection of 209 eggs displayed across London, it was voted the third “most popular egg” by the public.




Image 2: Installation
Pathways of Light

Title: Pathways of Light
Year made: 2022
Dimensions: 33 x 33 x 150 cm
Material: Installation art with broken Perspex mirror, eggshell, and light
This sculptural installation shares the title and theme with the proposed performance for the Whitechapel Gallery (2025). The image shows part of a larger installation exhibited at Central Saint Martins and was recognized with a prestigious LVMH Sustainability Award.




Image 3, performance

Hologram of the universe

Image 3
Title: Hologram of Our Universe
Year made: 2022
Duration: 3–4 minutes
Material: Digital and performance
At the 2022 Art and Science Symposium at Central Saint Martins, Hologram of Our Universe merged a 3D digital holographic projection with live human movement, blurring the lines between human and machine-led performance. Inspired by physics, this work explored the idea of the universe as a projection, shifting between cosmic forms and geometric abstractions. The performer’s movements interacted with the hologram, creating a dynamic dialogue that questioned identity, agency, and our evolving relationship with machines in the digital age.

Video 1 Title and File name: Rock Paper Scissors
Year made: 2022
Duration: 5 minutes
Material: Violin, charcoal, and algorithms that respond to body motion to create a digital image
A three-way collaboration: A violinist begins playing music. A charcoal artist responds to the music by drawing. An algorithm tracks her body movements, creating a digital image of snow dispersing on a screen, to which the violinist responds by altering his music, completing a dynamic loop of interaction.




Portrait of our universe, 2023
Cracked mirror and light. 35cmx35cm.

"Portrait of Our Universe" is a three-dimensional cracked mirror, encased in a box frame, and hung like a painting. Light shines through the cracks which appear as a web of interconnections. It is as if the boundaries between you and me blur when the light shines, making our individual identities indistinguishable from the magnificent portrait of our universe.
