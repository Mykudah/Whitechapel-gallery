Image 1: Sculpture and performance
Hiranya Garbha More Info

Title: Hiranya Garbha
Year made: 2012
Dimensions: 70 cm
Material: Sculpture with resin and paint, accompanied by a one-time performance

This sculpture was exhibited in Covent Garden for 40 days in 2012 and subsequently auctioned for charity. Before the auction, a live performance opposite St. Paul’s Churchyard featured the egg being painted in front of an audience. The performance symbolized birth. Part of a collection of 209 eggs displayed across London, it was voted the third “most popular egg” by the public.

Title: Pathways of Light
Year made: 2022

Material: Installation art with broken Perspex mirror, eggshell, and light

This sculptural installation shares the title and theme with the proposed performance for the Whitechapel Gallery (2025). The image shows part of a larger installation exhibited at Central Saint Martins and was recognized with a prestigious LVMH Sustainability Award.

Image 3: Performance
Hologram of Our Universe More Info

Title: Hologram of Our Universe
Year made: 2022
Duration: 3–4 minutes
Material: Digital and performance

At the 2022 Art and Science Symposium at Central Saint Martins, Hologram of Our Universe merged a 3D digital holographic projection with live human movement, blurring the lines between human and machine-led performance. Inspired by physics, this work explored the idea of the universe as a projection, shifting between cosmic forms and geometric abstractions.

Video 1: Live performance
Rock, Paper, Scissors More Info

Title: Rock, Paper, Scissors
Year made: 2022
Duration: 5 minutes
Material: Violin, charcoal, and algorithms that respond to body motion to create a digital image

A three-way collaboration: A violinist begins playing music. A charcoal artist responds to the music by drawing. An algorithm tracks her body movements, creating a digital image of snow dispersing on a screen, to which the violinist responds by altering his music, completing a dynamic loop of interaction.
